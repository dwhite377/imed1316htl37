About Wild Horses

Wild horses roam freely in vast grasslands and forests. Learn about their behavior, habitat, and conservation efforts.  Wild horses in the United States are a reintroduced native species that evolved on the North American continent millions of years ago.  They migrated across the Bering Land Bridge into Siberia during glacial periods, but went locally extinct around 12,000 years ago.  In the 1500s, Spanish conquistadors brought Europeans horses back to North American, where they quickly adapted to the open grasslands and spread across the continent.  Some of these horses escaped and bred with horses already living in North America with Native Americans, becoming the free-roaming mustangs of the American West.  By the mid 1800s, there were millions of wild mustangs roaming the United States.
